About me

I am a second year PhD student and NSF graduate research fellow working with Allison Strom at Northwestern University.

Click here for my CV

Massive stars

I am interested the evolution and properties of massive stars, binary evolution, and how it is affected by metallicity.

High-z galaxies

Massive stars at high redshift are observationally distinct from those in the local universe, and accurately modeling their radiation is crucial to interpreting the new observations of high-redshift galaxies from JWST.

Models

I work with MESA and multiple stellar population synthesis codes. I am interested in comparing existing models with different treatments of binary stars to observations and investigating how we can improve these models to better reproduce observations.
